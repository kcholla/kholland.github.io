U-tour

UX Design Concept Project

iOS application to connect touring artists with venues and help route tours.

Recognize the Problem

Booking live shows can be difficult without connections.

Venues like to know they can trust musicians to bring a crowd.

Touring artists and bands sign with agencies to book live music.

This can be expensive, not to mention, uncommon and difficult for new artists.

Booking multiple shows in different places, dates, and times can be difficult to organize and maintain.

The Challenge

Design an iOS application to connect touring artists with venues and to help route tours.

Utour gives users direct access to new venues in need of live music while also supporting artists in a competitive industry.

Understand the Users

I surveyed 21 touring musicians to find out different ways users search for venues.

I needed to find out the following:

  • Do touring muscians book their own shows?
  • What do touring muscians look for when they are searching for venues?
  • How do touring musicians route tours?

I designed and conducted a survey to answer these questions and to collect information based on potential users.

Know the Competition

I conducted a competitive analysis to understand what other kinds of booking tools were offered to musicians. I found that the tools that do exist mostly focus on management vs. booking live shows.

The Solution

Create an iOS application, U-tour, to connect artists with venues and help route tours. U-tour gives users direct access to new venues looking for live music and supports artist by providing a platform to participate in a competitive industry.

Designing the Experience

After nailing down user stories, I designed user flows to account for steps in creating low-fidelity wireframes.

Lo-Fi Wireframes

Using Sketch, I designed low-fidelity wireframes for user flows, and then created a clickable prototype for a first round of usability testing.

Users had a difficult time adding the date. Users continuosly clicked on the date's number on the calendar to add an event. This lead to adding a feature allowing the user to click a date to show it's events, and then add an event.

Users also did not understand how to add or create a new tour. I created more clear CTA buttons so that users could more easily understand the action of creating a tour.

Hi-Fi Mockups & User Testing

  • The new CTA to add an event after clicking the date proved helpful, and users were able to easily add a new date.
  • Larger and more obvious "create tour" CTA allowed users to know how to create a new tour and save a tour.
  • Updated copy in search bars helped users understand actions for searching venues and adding locations.

Continual Work

  • Research venues to understand what they need to connect with artists.
  • Implement how venues use Utour to connect with artists.
  • Create a messageing system within the app, thus moving away from email.
  • Make Utour mutually beneficial for artists and venues.
